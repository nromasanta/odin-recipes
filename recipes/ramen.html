Ramen

Description

Ramen is a Japanese noodle dish, consisting of Chinese style alkaline wheat noodles served in a meat-based broth, often flavored with soy sauce or miso and uses toppings such as pork, nori (dried seaweed), and scallions.

Ramen has roots in Chinese noodle dishes. Nearly every region in Japan has its own variation of rmaen, such as tonkotsu (pork bone broth) and miso ramen.

Ingredients

  • 3.5 cups of vegetable broth
  • 1 package of ramen noodles with dried vegetables
  • 2 teaspoons of soy sauce
  • 1/2 teaspoon chili oil
  • 1 teaspoon seasame oil
  • 2 green onions, sliced

Steps

  1. In medium saucepan, combine both broth and noodles
  2. Cover and bring to a boil over high heat, stir to break up noodles
  3. Reduce heat to medium and add soy sauce, chili oil, and ginger
  4. Simmer, uncovered, for 10 minutes
  5. Stir in sesame oil and garnish with green onion
